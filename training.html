How to Label Hair Images

This page explains everything you need to know to label photos of hair consistently and accurately for our machine learning dataset. Please read through once before starting, then keep it open while labeling.

Section 1

Overview & Goal

Firstly, thank you for helping us with our project! You will be helping us build a training dataset for an AI model that will be able to accuratley identify and generate historically underrepresented hair types. Our priority is to label what is visible in each image. If you are unsure, please choose Unsure/Not Visible rather than guessing. Consistency beats precision!

Primary goal:
  • Classify hair characteristics (curl radius, twist frequency, curl wavelength, porosity, thickness).
Important:
  • Base labels on the current photo only. Don't infer by asking other people or online resources.
  • Skip the image when the view is obstructed or out of frame.

Section 3

Labeling Instructions

  1. Scan the image for lighting and determine whether enough hair is visible for you to make a well-informed decision about the hair.
  2. Choose curl diameter, twist frequency, curl wavelength using your best judgement, making estimates based on the scale of the person's face.
  3. Choose porosity and thickness (of each individual strand, not of the hair as a whole). You may need to use context clues to determine porosity (i.e. how frizzy the hair looks).
  4. Skip the image if it is blurry, the hair is blocked, etc.
Section 4

Quality Rules & Edge Cases

Label ✅
  • Clear view of hair with acceptable lighting.
  • Protective styles (braids, twists, locs) — only if you are able to make a well-informed judgement based on the parts of the hair that are visible.
Do NOT label ❌
  • Blurry/blocked hair where core attributes are not visible.
  • Images where you aren't able to make a judgement about the scale of the hair because not enough context clues are visible.

Reviewer rubric (self‑check):
  • Accuracy: Labels match visible evidence; no guessing.
  • Completeness: All applicable fields filled.
  • Consistency: Same judgement applied across all images.
